To The Moon (WIP Title)

Final group project for my Unity Class, a full game using what we learned. The main concept was my idea.

Story:
In the future, there is too much space debris in our atmosphere that it prevents space travel.
NASA has to send a robot up to the moon to clear the debris.
It will use a gizmo in its legs/feet to hop from cloud to cloud into the sky.

Instructions:

  1. Use WASD to navigate through the scene.
  2. Use the spacebar to jump into the sky.
  3. White clouds are safe to land on.
  4. Storm clouds are dangerous, landing on them will mean taking damage.
  5. After a certain height, there will be some clouds that you can fall through.
  6. To return the menu, hit Escape or run out of health.
  7. Try to get as high in the sky as possible!

Member Contributions:

  • Jordan (me): Game idea and plot, randomize platforms script, cloud movement script, damage on collision, piecing project together, assist Sayem with Terrain, asset modeling (clouds, launchpad with support), audio implementation, project documentation
  • Dan: Background assets (rockets, buildings)
  • Sayem: Terrain & trees
  • Valerian: UI Elements, start menu, player damage script, player height script, menu script

3D Campus HW - Pokémon Ranger School

For a homework assignment for my Unity class, we had the task to design and develop a 3D campus scene using what we had recently learned.
The requirements for the assignment are as follows:

  • Design and create a 3D campus environment. The elements for the campus include buildings, trees, landscape, grasses, playground, etc.
  • Create dynamic water (lake/pond/river) by yourself, a screenshot of all wired nodes in the water shader is needed
  • Add directional light
  • Add point light
  • Add spotlight
  • Add two or more area lights
  • Add the first-person character from the Standard Assets and modify the C# code to make the first-person game runnable
  • Use two cameras: one for the player (this one is already included in the first-person character) and another one showing the map location of the player in real time)
  • Add water sound to the dynamic water object. Play the sound every time the player approaches the water
  • Add background music to your game

For this project, since a specific campus was not assigned, I decided to make my own redition of the school campus in the 2008 Nintendo DS game Pokemon Ranger: Shadows of Almia.
I included the main school building, the training hall, obstacles in the field students train on, and the monument Pledge Stone by the docks.

Halloween Village

For a homework assignment for my Unity class, we had the task to create a halloween themed scene using the certain tools we learned recently.
The requirements for the assignment are as follows:

  • Your project must have the Halloween ambiance
  • You can create a Terrain with a size (width*Length) of 600*600 by Heightmap Sculpting or Sculpting tools
  • You can use the Raise or Lower Terrain tool
  • You can use the Smooth Height tool
  • You can use the Paint Texture tools to create at least two different terrain layers with proper textures, normal maps, and tiling settings
  • You can create at least two trees and paint them on the terrain.
  • You can paint grasses on the terrain.
  • You can create a simple lake with colors.

At the same time of the assignment, a new season of a show I like called The Owl House came out, and I got an idea for the assignment.

I made a culdesac with a bunch of houses along the road. I made the scene nighttime and added streetlights, pumpkins, and jack-0-lanterns for the Halloween ambiance.
I surrounded the culdesac in trees and had a lake scene based on a scene from The Owl House hidden in the forest along a dirt path.

Stanley Parable Parody

This was finished project I made for my final group project in my Unreal Engine class.
We were split into groups and could create whatever project we wanted for the assignment.
My group and I decided we wanted to try to create a parody of the game The Stanley Parable, as it had be recently rereleased with extra content.

I created most of the models in the project, including the computer monitor, mouse, keyboard, desk, chair, and doors.
I also assisted in finding textures for other assets, consulting on the map layout and script lines.
Another group member, my friend Katie, she lead the project by making the map layout and script as well as piecing together the project on her computer.
Another group member, Chris, created a filing cabinet and a trash can model to use in the scene.
The last group member, Victor, only checked to see if our textures could be used on materials in a seperate Unreal Engine project.
I managed to get my friend Adam to voice the lines in the script since he did a funny impression of the Narrator from the original game.
